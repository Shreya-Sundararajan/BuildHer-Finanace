Resources & Investing Options

Learn about financial resources and investment opportunities for young entrepreneurs

üìö Educational Resources

  • Girls Who Code - Free coding programs and clubs for girls
  • Khan Academy Finance - Free courses on personal and business finance
  • MIT OpenCourseWare - Free STEM and entrepreneurship courses
  • Code.org - Learn computer science fundamentals
  • Investopedia - Financial education and terminology

üéØ Startup Resources

  • Startup Summer Programs - For high school students interested in entrepreneurship
  • Local Incubators - Check for youth-focused startup incubators in your area
  • Small Business Development Centers - Government-supported business counseling
  • SCORE Mentoring - Free business mentoring from experienced professionals
  • Young Entrepreneur Council - Networking and resources for young founders

üí° Funding Opportunities

  • Scholarships for STEM - Many organizations offer STEM-specific scholarships
  • Youth Business Competitions - Pitch your idea for prize money
  • Crowdfunding Platforms - Kickstarter, Indiegogo for early-stage funding
  • Grants for Social Impact - Organizations that fund socially-conscious startups
  • University Programs - Many colleges have funding for student startups

üìä Types of Investments

üå± Seed Funding

Beginner Level

Early-stage investment for brand new startups. Typically comes from friends, family, or angel investors.

Potential Return: 5-10x

üöÄ Venture Capital

Advanced Level

Professional investment firms that fund high-growth startups in exchange for equity.

Potential Return: 10-30x

ü§ù Angel Investors

Intermediate Level

Wealthy individuals who invest in startups, often providing mentorship along with capital.

Potential Return: 8-15x

üè¢ Corporate Investment

Advanced Level

Large companies investing in startups that complement their business or technology.

Potential Return: 5-12x

üí° Investment Tips for Young Founders

  • Focus on building value before seeking investment
  • Only raise what you need - too much money can be as challenging as too little
  • Choose investors who offer mentorship, not just money
  • Understand that equity is ownership - giving up 20% means you keep 80%
  • Prepare a clear pitch deck showing your market, team, and financial projections
